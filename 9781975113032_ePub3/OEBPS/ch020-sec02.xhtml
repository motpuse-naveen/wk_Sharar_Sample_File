II. Patient History and Physical Examination

Airway management always begins with a thorough airway-relevant history, including a search for documentation of airway-related events during previous anesthetics. Signs and symptoms related to potentially difficult airway management, including aspiration risk, should be sought (Table 20.2) as many congenital and acquired syndromes are associated with difficult airway management (Table 20.3).
