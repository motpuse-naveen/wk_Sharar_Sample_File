Anesthesia for Thoracic Surgery

Katherine Marseu and Peter Slinger

The most common indication for thoracic surgery is malignancy.1-3 Despite this, a wide variety of pathologies and procedures are commonly encountered when providing anesthetics for patients undergoing thoracic surgery. As a result, there are a number of important preoperative, intraoperative, and postoperative anesthetic considerations for thoracic surgery.
