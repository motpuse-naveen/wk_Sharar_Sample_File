I. Ocular Anatomy

The eye is formed by the orbit, globe, extraocular muscles, eyelid, and lacrimal system (Figure 29.4).

The outer fibrous layer forms the sclera, cornea, and corneoscleral junction. The middle layer is formed by the choroid, ciliary body, ciliary processes, and the iris. The choroid contains a dense vascular bed, while the ciliary body controls lens thickness. The pupil is an aperture, the diameter of which is controlled by the sphincter pupillae (parasympathetic innervation) and dilator pupillae (sympathetic innervation). The former constricts the pupil and the latter dilates it.
